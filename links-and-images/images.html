Images

This page covers common image formats, but you may also be looking for links and useful extras

JPGs

JPG images are good for photos

  • JPG images are designed for handling large color palettes without exorbitantly increasing file size
  • This makes them great for photos and images with lots of gradients
  • They can't have transparent pixels, though.

GIFs

GIFs are good for animations

  • GIFs are good for simple animations, but they have a limited color palette (don't use them for photos)
  • Transparent pixels are also binary (either transparent or non-transparent), which means you can't have semi-opaque pixels
  • Use a PNG if you don't need the image to be animated.

PNGs

PNGs are good for diagrams and icons

  • PNGs are great for anything that's not a photo or animated
  • A PNG of the same quality (as perceived by the human eye) is generally larger than an equivalent JPG file
  • They handle opacity and transparency well, and don't have color palette limitations - making them excellent for icons, technical diagrams, logos, etc.

SVGs

SVGs are amazing. Use them wherever you can

  • SVGs are a vector-based graphics format, which means that they can scale to any dimension without a loss of quality
  • This maeks them great for responsive design
  • They do everything PNGs do, but better.

The only disadvantage of svgs is that text fields need to be converted to outlines w/ Adobe Illustrator / Sketch. This significantly increases the file sizes of images with lots of text
